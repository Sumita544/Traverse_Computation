Traverse

Traversing is a method of surveying where a series of connected lines, known as traverse lines, are established to determine the lengths and directions of these lines. These lines form a framework, and the directions and lengths are measured using an angle measuring instrument (such as a theodolite or compass) and a tape or chain, respectively.

Show More

Traverse computations are essential in various engineering and construction projects, as they provide a basis for mapping, layout, and control. Advanced techniques like total stations and GPS are often used to increase the precision of traverse measurements.

Type of Traverse:
Open Traverse:Such traverse which is geometrically as well as mathematicallty open. Suitable for surveying long, narrow strips of land, such as roads, canals, or coastlines.
Closed Traverse:Such traverse which is geometrically as well as mathematically closed. Often used for surveys of large areas, such as lakes, woods, or boundaries.
Link Traverse:Such traverse which is geometrically open mathematically close.
